Composition + Color = Emotion

How proportion and palette combine to shape psychological response in masterworks and modern media

La La Land (2016)

Mia is coded in blue — longing, creativity, reality. Sebastian in red — passion, jazz, ambition. Their moments of harmony appear in purple.

Vibrant triadic palettes in spring/summer give way to muted, desaturated tones as dreams collide with reality.

Claude Monet — Water Lilies

Analogous cool tones and soft, rhythmic composition produce meditative calm and introspection.

Vincent van Gogh — Starry Night

Complementary yellow-blue contrast combined with swirling, rhythmic movement evokes wonder, restlessness, and cosmic awe.

Jaws (1975) Poster

Low-key darkness pierced by sharp complementary red creates immediate primal tension and dread.

Coca-Cola Classic Campaigns

Warm, high-energy palette combined with symmetrical, balanced composition produces instant feelings of joy, refreshment, and social connection.
