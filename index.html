Color Psychology

Primary Color Systems: RYB vs RGB vs CMYK

RYB (Red-Yellow-Blue)

Traditional artist model (subtractive – pigments/paint). Used for centuries in painting. Mixing all three → muddy brown/black.

Best for: physical art, traditional color theory

RGB (Red-Green-Blue)

Additive model (light). Screens, digital art, photography. Mixing all three at full intensity → white.

Best for: web, monitors, video, digital design

CMYK (Cyan-Magenta-Yellow-Key/Black)

Subtractive model (ink/print). Printing & physical output. Mixing all three → dark (black added for true black).

Best for: posters, books, packaging

RYB mixing (paint)

RGB additive (light mixing)

CMYK subtractive (ink mixing)
